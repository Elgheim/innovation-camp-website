Koonan, Ivory Coast: Community School

In Koonan elemtary school, Ivory Coast, there was put in place a trial project, where the aim was to include parants and community as much as possible. Starting times were adjusted to fit the majority. Twice a week parants that had time, could come and eat lunsj with their children. The project startet 8 months ago and thanks to everyone at Koonan elemtary school, we get rapports that drop-out rate has fallen 83% and the parents loves seeing their children happy at school. We are now looking to increase this project to other schools.

At Save the Children we belive that every child deserves the opurtunity to go to school. We're working hard across the world, so that every child can be able to learn and grow. At the core, these new projects aim to learn more about effective meassurements to decrease dropouts.

Sunyani, Ghana: Trying something new

In Sunyani Elementary School, Ghana, there is a strong tradition of siesta and usualy the parants are home around noone, when the sun is too strong. The last three months, the school has rearranged their time schedual, and implemented a two hours break around noone. Three hours school, two hours siesta and three hours school again. It's to early to see anything regarding statistics, but we have gotten repports about an increase in focus and happiness among the students.

Sokode, Togo: Building ownership

A key part of keeping children in school, might be increasing parent ownership to the school. In Sokode, Togo, the community in cooporation with the school, decidet to start a yearly maintenance day. All the community gathered to paint, fix and help the school be in top state before schoolstart. Further started they a monthly barbeque at the school grounds. Sokode Elementary School stands out, not only in Mali, but in the whole region.
